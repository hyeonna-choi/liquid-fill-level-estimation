A fully synthetic, multi-view RGB-D pipeline estimates the liquid fill level inside dual-transparent labware — where both the container and the liquid are see-through.

Abstract

Estimating how much liquid sits inside transparent laboratory glassware is a core perception step for autonomous biological experiments, yet it is notoriously hard: both the container and the liquid are transparent, refraction distorts the scene, and manual annotation of fill levels is impractical at scale.

We build an automated synthetic data generation pipeline in Blender that renders dual-transparent labware with randomized camera poses, HDRI backgrounds, and fill levels from 1–100%, producing multi-view RGB-D images with exact ground-truth labels for free. We then train a Vision Transformer (ViT) that fuses multi-view RGB-D inputs to regress the liquid fill level, transferring from purely synthetic data.

Method

🖼️ static/images/pipeline.png — 전체 파이프라인 구조도 (Blender 렌더링 → multi-view RGB-D → ViT → fill-level)
  1. Synthetic generation (Blender Python): procedurally randomized camera poses, HDRI lighting, labware geometry, and liquid fill levels (1–100%).
  2. Multi-view RGB-D rendering: paired color + depth from several viewpoints, with pixel-perfect fill-level ground truth.
  3. ViT estimator: a transformer fuses the multi-view RGB-D tokens and regresses the fill level.
